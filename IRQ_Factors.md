# Internal Representations Questionnaire (IRQ)

From Roebuck & Lupyan (2020)

## Factor 1: Visual Representations

1. I often enjoy the use of mental pictures to reminisce
2. I can close my eyes and easily picture a scene that I have experienced
3. My mental images are very vivid and photographic
4. The old saying "A picture is worth a thousand words" is certainly true for me
5. When I think about someone I know well, I instantly see their face in my mind
6. I often use mental images or pictures to help me remember things
7. My memories are mainly visual in nature
8. When traveling to get to somewhere I tend to think more visually than verbally
9. If I talk to myself in my head it is usually accompanied by visual imagery
10. If I imagine my memories visually they are more often moving than static

## Factor 2: Verbal Representations (Internal Voice)

1. When I think about someone I know well, I instantly hear their voice in my mind
2. I think about problems in my mind in the form of a conversation with myself
3. If I am walking somewhere by myself, I often have a silent conversation with myself
4. If I am walking somewhere by myself, I frequently think of conversations that I've recently had
5. My inner speech helps my imagination
6. I tend to think things through verbally when I am relaxing
7. When thinking about a social problem, I often talk it through in my head
8. I like to give myself some down time to talk through thoughts in my mind
9. I hear words in my "mind's ear" when I think
10. I rarely vocalize thoughts in my mind *
11. I often talk to myself internally while watching TV
12. My memories often involve conversations I've had

## Factor 3: Mental Manipulation

1. I can easily imagine and mentally rotate three-dimensional geometric figures
2. I can easily choose to imagine this sentence in my mind pronounced unnaturally slowly
3. In school, I had no problems with geometry
4. It is easy for me to imagine the sensation of licking a brick
5. I find it difficult to imagine how a three-dimensional geometric figure would exactly look like when rotated *
6. I can easily imagine someone clearly talking, and then imagine the same voice with a heavy cold
7. I think I have a large vocabulary in my native language compared to others
8. I can easily imagine the sound of a trumpet getting louder

## Factor 4: Written/Text Representations (Orthographic Imagery)

1. I find it easy to decide if words rhyme by seeing their spelling in my mind's eye
2. When I hear someone talking, I see words written down in my mind
3. I see words in my "mind's eye" when I think
4. When I am introduced to someone for the first time, I imagine what their name would look like when written down
5. A strategy I use to help me remember written material is imagining what the writing looks like
6. I hear a running summary of everything I am doing in my head
7. I rehearse in my mind how someone might respond to a text message before I send it

---

\* Items are reverse coded
